The main advantages of a guided busway, versus a normal road are higher speeds (meaning increased capacity) and increased safety as traffic of differing directions is physically separated.
www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-14405734
The world's longest guided busway has opened in Cambridge.  The busway provides a separate path for buses away from normal traffic, takes up less room than a road and allows buses to rejoin other vehicles for routes through the city centre.  But opponents say the scheme is two years late, massively over budget and should have been a rail link instead.  Our transport correspondent, Richard Scott, went for a ride to find out how it works.
